About the Program

Training Arizona’s Next Semiconductor Engineers

The Intel CHIPS Scholarship & Fellowship Program funds a 12-student Master’s cohort hosted by NAU’s Steve Sanghi College of Engineering in collaboration with the University of Arizona. Students are selected by Intel and the Semiconductor Research Corporation against semiconductor workforce priorities, with the cohort launching in Academic Year 2026–27.

The program targets three pillars of Intel’s Arizona operations: advanced packaging and heterogeneous integration, manufacturing process metrology, and co-packaged optics. Scholars are drawn from Mechanical Engineering, Electrical and Computer Engineering, and Optical Engineering. NAU contributes nine scholars and the University of Arizona three, with cross-campus co-advising and facility access built into every plan of study.

NAU brings the MPaCT Lab — a 25+ instrument fabrication and metrology facility — alongside an established TSMC apprenticeship pipeline and the Sanghi College’s accelerated BS⇒MS pathways. UofA brings the Wyant College of Optical Sciences and the Center for Semiconductor Manufacturing, staffed in part by former Intel engineers, with two decades of SRC-affiliated graduate training.
