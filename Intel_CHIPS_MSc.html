About the Program

Training Arizona’s Next Semiconductor Engineers

The Intel CHIPS Scholarship & Fellowship Program funds a 12-student Master’s cohort hosted by NAU’s Steve Sanghi College of Engineering in collaboration with the University of Arizona. Students are selected by Intel and the Semiconductor Research Corporation against semiconductor workforce priorities, with the cohort launching in Academic Year 2026–27.

The program targets three pillars of Intel’s Arizona operations: advanced packaging and heterogeneous integration, manufacturing process metrology, and co-packaged optics. Scholars are drawn from Mechanical Engineering, Electrical and Computer Engineering, and Optical Engineering. NAU contributes nine scholars and the University of Arizona three, with cross-campus co-advising and facility access built into every plan of study.

NAU brings the MPaCT Lab — a 25+ instrument fabrication and metrology facility — alongside an established TSMC apprenticeship pipeline and the Sanghi College’s accelerated BS⇒MS pathways. UofA brings the Wyant College of Optical Sciences and the Center for Semiconductor Manufacturing, staffed in part by former Intel engineers, with two decades of SRC-affiliated graduate training.

Research Pillars

Where Scholars Will Work

Every Intel Scholar focuses on one of four research pillars aligned with Intel’s Microelectronics and Advanced Packaging Technologies (MAPT) roadmap. Two cohort tracks keep the work degree-aligned: Manufacturing & Metrology (Mechanical Engineering), and Electronics, CPO & Hardware (ECE and Optical Engineering).

01
Metrology & Inspection In-fab and near-fab measurement, defect detection, and yield analysis using MPaCT’s 25+ instrument suite.
02
Precision Manufacturing & Packaging Fine-pitch assembly, chemical-mechanical planarization, thermocompression bonding, and packaging automation.
03
Materials Reliability Interconnect fatigue, thermal cycling, failure analysis, and 3D heterogeneous integration characterization.
04
Co-Packaged Optics (CPO) Fiber-attach, optical engine integration, and silicon photonics work at UofA’s photonics cleanroom and labs.
Scholars are co-advised by NAU and UofA faculty, with Intel champion engineers across each pillar. Cross-institution facility access is bidirectional — NAU students work at UofA labs and vice versa.
Your Path

How It Works

From registering your interest to placing into an Intel-aligned semiconductor role — in three stages.

1

Register Your Interest

Submit the registration form below with your current institution, intended major, and degree interest (MS or PhD). Open to incoming and onboard MS students who are US Citizens, Nationals, or Permanent Residents.

2

Match & Enroll

Selected scholars are matched to a research pillar and to NAU or UofA faculty advisors. Cohorts launch Fall 2026, with optional Spring 2027 and Fall 2027 entry windows for the staggered cohort.

3

Research, Mentor, Place

Complete a thesis or industry-aligned project, an annual Intel facility visit, and an internship in Manufacturing, Silicon Hardware, or Semiconductor Research — with placement targeted into the semiconductor workforce or a PhD program.
