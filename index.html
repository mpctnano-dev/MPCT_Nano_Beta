Welcome to the NAU Nanofabrication Core Facility. We are the premier open-access research hub in Northern Arizona, dedicated to transforming academic theory into industrial reality.

As a central pillar of Arizona’s emerging semiconductor corridor, we provide state-of-the-art lithography, deposition, and characterization tools. Our mission is to cultivate a transdisciplinary ecosystem where students, faculty, and industry partners collaborate to push the boundaries of materials science and device engineering.

Integrated Education Paths

Academics →

Technician & Workforce Training

Workforce Ready

A fast-track program designed for immediate entry into the semiconductor workforce. This path focuses heavily on equipment operation, safety protocols, and cleanroom maintenance. Students leave with certifications recognized by major industry partners.

Key Competencies:

  • ISO 5 Cleanroom Protocols
  • Chemical Handling & Safety
  • Basic Metrology & Inspection
View Course Catalog

Undergraduate Engineering Labs

Physics & Engineering

Integrating real-world lab experience into engineering and physics curricula. This path bridges the gap between textbook theory and physical application. Students design, build, and test their own micro-devices during capstone projects.

Core Curriculum:

  • Solid State Physics
  • Electronic Materials
  • Device Characterization
View Degree Plans

Graduate Research & Development

Advanced Degrees

Supporting Master’s and PhD students with the sophisticated tools needed for novel material discovery. This path offers 24/7 facility access, advanced training on lithography and deposition systems, and grant writing support.

Research Areas:

  • Quantum Dot Synthesis
  • MEMS / NEMS Design
  • Photonic Integrated Circuits
