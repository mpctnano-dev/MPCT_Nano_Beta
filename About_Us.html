Shared-Use Facility

Your Lab. Without the Overhead.

The MPaCT Lab operates as a shared-use facility — modeled after the nation's leading university research centers. Rather than locking advanced instrumentation behind departmental walls, we centralize world-class tools and expert staff under one roof, making them accessible to anyone who needs them.

Whether you're an NAU undergraduate running your first SEM analysis, a faculty researcher characterizing novel thin films, or an industry engineer validating a new process — the MPaCT Lab is your facility. We handle the calibration, maintenance, and training so you can focus on the science.

Our Mission

The NAU Microelectronics Processing and Characterization Testing Lab (MPaCT) is a central pillar of Arizona's emerging semiconductor corridor. We provide state-of-the-art metrology, lithography, deposition, and characterization tools that serve as the backbone for transdisciplinary research and workforce development.

Our mission is to cultivate an ecosystem where students, faculty, and industry partners collaborate to push the boundaries of materials science and device engineering — bridging the gap between the classroom and the cleanroom.

Cite Our Facility

If your research utilized the MPaCT Lab's equipment or staff support, we kindly request that you include the following acknowledgement in your publications:

"This work was performed in part at the Microelectronics Processing and Characterization Testing Lab (MPaCT) at Northern Arizona University, Flagstaff, AZ."

Proper acknowledgement helps us demonstrate the impact of shared-use facilities and secure continued funding for equipment and staff.
